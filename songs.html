The Grogan & Brogan Manifesto

A Ballad in Prose

We, Grogan & Brogan, Irish balladeers of the modern diaspora, hereby commit ourselves to ...

1. Songs That Remember.

We sing for the emigrants, the navvies, the ghost sons — the lost lovers, the unnamed dead. If a man died at sea, if a child never came home, if a woman held her family together with prayer and porter — we will sing their story.

2. The Ridiculous and the Sacred.

In our world, a rodent in a lunchbox sits beside a famine lament. A drunken bear delivers theology. A donkey becomes a prophet. We laugh because it’s our inheritance. We weep because we remember.

3. Dissonance and Devotion.

Grogan writes. Brogan riffs. We argue, we harmonize, we get it wrong and we get it right. That’s the duet. That’s the magic.

4. Invention, Not Imitation.

We honour tradition, but we do not freeze it. We echo the sean-nós, the wake-room poet, the kitchen revolutionary — but we speak to now.

5. Satire is a Sacrament.

From “Drunken Paddy” to “Rodent in the Lunchbox,” we never separate mockery from meaning. Satire is our love language. Irreverence is a form of reverence.

6. The Song Isn’t Finished.

Brogan vanished. Grogan stayed. But the project sings on. The donkey hasn’t brayed its final note. The stash still overflows.

7. We Bring the Songs Home.

To Ireland. To Luxembourg. To Africa. To hall and hearth, schoolyard and shoreline. If there are ears, we will sing. If there is silence, we’ll fill it.

8. The Stash Is Sacred.

These are not just songs. They are lived truths. “The Exile’s Lament,” “Sleep Well Lads,” “England,” “The Artist” — this is our shared heartbeat.

9. We Remember Brogan.

Wherever he is — Donegal? Zanzibar? Another melodic dimension? His spirit is in every chord, every silence, every wrong note made true.

10. We Are Not Finished.

The fire is lit. The stories keep arriving. The donkey keeps braying. The next verse waits.

Signed in song,
Grogan (for now)
& the absent but ever-present Brogan
